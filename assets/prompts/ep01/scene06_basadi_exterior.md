## Prompt — v1

```
Wide shot photograph of a modest ancient Jain temple (basadi) exterior in harsh midday tropical sunlight. The building has dressed grey-black basalt stone walls, a stepped pyramidal tower (not a tall gopuram), and a pillared entrance porch with deep shadow under its overhang. Carved tirthankara figures are faintly visible on the facade. The architecture is dignified but restrained — this is a Jain temple, not a Hindu royal temple. No ornate decoration.

Hard overhead tropical sunlight creates extreme contrast: the stone surfaces blazing in the sun, deep black shadows under the porch, eaves, and overhangs.

Three depth planes:
- Foreground: a pair of royal guards flanking the path, spears held upright, casting long shadows on red-brown laterite earth. Simple military dress, no Mughal influence.
- Middle ground: a solitary figure in white — Vishvaksena — walking alone toward the basadi entrance. His white cotton dhoti is the brightest element in the frame, almost blinding in the sun. Shaved head visible. He walks with purpose.
- Background: the basadi facade with its dark doorway. A sagging cotton awning over a nearby market stall. Dust in the hot air.

The ground is dry red laterite earth, cracked from heat. Scrubby neem trees with sparse foliage. No lush green vegetation. This is hot-season Deccan — 40 degrees, dry, dusty.

Color palette: Temple Stone earth, grey-black basalt, Monsoon Ivory sky (pale warm, NOT blue), blinding white dhoti, Lamp Black shadows. High contrast, minimal midtones.
```
